## Chicken Curry

A simple, comforting chicken curry with a well-rounded masala, not too oily, not too spicy. Perfect with steamed rice or rotis.

---

### Ingredients (2-3 servings)

#### Chicken

- 500 g **chicken** (bone-in preferred)
- Salt, to taste
- 1/2 tsp turmeric powder

#### Curry Base

- 3 tbsp oil
- 2 medium **onions**, finely chopped
- 1 tbsp **ginger–garlic paste**
- 2 medium **tomatoes**, finely chopped or pureed
- 1 tsp **red chilli powder** (adjust to taste)
- 1 tsp **coriander powder**
- 1/2 tsp **cumin powder**
- 1/2 tsp **garam masala**
- 1/2 tsp **black pepper powder** (optional)
- Salt, to taste

#### Whole Spices (optional but recommended)

- 1 bay leaf
- 2 green cardamoms
- 1 small cinnamon stick

#### Finish

- Water, as needed
- Fresh coriander leaves, chopped

---

### Method

#### 1. Marinate the chicken

1. Mix 2-3 tbsp of curd with 1 tsp of chilli powder and salt to taste, with your chicken.
2. Let it rest for 2-3 hours, preferably overnight.

#### 2. Prepare the base

1. Heat oil in a deep pan on medium heat.
2. Add whole spices and let them release their aroma.
3. Add chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.

#### 3. Build the masala

1. Add ginger–garlic paste and sauté until the raw smell disappears.
2. Add tomatoes and cook until soft and oil begins to separate.
3. Add chilli powder, coriander powder, cumin powder, turmeric, pepper, and salt.
4. Cook the masala well on medium-low heat until thick and glossy.

#### 4. Cook the chicken

1. Add chicken pieces and mix well to coat with the masala.
2. Cook uncovered for 5–7 minutes, letting the chicken release its juices.
3. Add water to reach your preferred gravy consistency.
4. Cover and simmer for 15–20 minutes until chicken is tender.

#### 5. Finish

1. Add garam masala and mix gently.
2. Simmer uncovered for 2–3 minutes.
3. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.

---

### Serving Suggestions

- Serve hot with **steamed rice**, **jeera rice**, or **roti/naan**.
- A side of **onion salad or pickle** pairs perfectly.

---

### Notes

- Bone-in chicken gives better depth of flavor.
- For a thicker curry, reduce water and cook longer uncovered.
- Tastes even better the next day as the masala settles.
