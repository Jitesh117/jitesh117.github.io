# Peri Peri Chicken Popcorn

Crispy **peri peri chicken popcorn** — crunchy outside, juicy inside, tossed in a bold, tangy peri peri coating.

---

## Ingredients

### Chicken

- 200–250 g **boneless chicken** (thighs or breast), cut into bite-sized pieces
- Salt, to taste
- Black pepper, to taste
- Juice of 1 lemon _(or 2 tbsp curd)_
- 1 tsp chilli powder

### Dry Batter

- quarter cup **cornflour (cornstarch)**
- one cup **all-purpose flour (maida)**
- 1 tsp coriander powder
- 1 tsp onion powder
- Black pepper, to taste
- 1 tsp salt

### For Frying

- Oil, for deep or shallow frying

### Peri Peri Toss

- 1½–2 tbsp peri peri seasoning
- 1 tbsp butter or olive oil
- 1 tsp lemon juice
- 1 tsp garlic powder _(optional)_

---

## Step 1: Prepare the Chicken

1. Add chicken to a bowl with salt, black pepper, chilli powder, and lemon juice.
2. Mix well, cover, and **marinate for 12 hours** in the refrigerator.

---

## Step 2: Coat the Chicken (Crunch Method)

1. In a bowl or container, mix cornflour, maida, coriander powder, onion powder, salt, and black pepper.
2. Add marinated chicken pieces to the dry batter **3–4 pieces at a time**.
3. Close the container and **shake well** until coated.
4. Transfer the coated chicken to a bowl of **cold water** for 30 seconds.
5. Remove from water and place back into the dry batter.
6. Shake again to form rough, flaky coating.
7. Place coated chicken on a plate and repeat for remaining pieces.

This double-dip method creates the signature popcorn-style crunch.

---

## Step 3: Fry the Chicken

1. Heat oil to **170–180°C (medium-hot)**.
2. Fry chicken in batches — do not overcrowd.
3. Turn occasionally and fry until **golden, crisp, and cooked through**.
4. Remove and rest on a wire rack or paper towel.

---

## Step 4: Peri Peri Toss

1. In a bowl, melt butter or warm olive oil slightly.
2. Add peri peri seasoning, lemon juice, and garlic powder.
3. Toss hot fried chicken popcorn in the mixture until evenly coated.

---

## Serving Suggestions

Serve immediately with:

- garlic mayo or peri peri mayo
- cold soda or beer
- fries or just on its own

---

## Notes

- Chicken thighs stay juicier, especially for longer frying.
- Adjust peri peri quantity based on heat tolerance.
- For extra crunch, rest battered chicken for 5 minutes before frying.
