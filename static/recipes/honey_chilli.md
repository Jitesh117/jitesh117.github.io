# Chicken Tenders (Regular & Honey Chilli)

Crispy, juicy **chicken tenders** with two finishes:

- **Regular crispy tenders** — clean, crunchy, dip-friendly
- **Honey chilli tenders** — sticky, sweet-heat glaze using honey, gochujang & soy

---

## Ingredients

### Chicken

- 250–300 g **chicken tenders** or boneless chicken breast, cut into strips
- Salt, to taste
- Black pepper, to taste
- 1 tsp chilli powder or paprika
- Juice of ½ lemon _(or 2 tbsp curd)_

### Dry Batter (Crispy Coating)

- quarter cup **cornflour (cornstarch)**
- one cup **all-purpose flour (maida)**
- 1 tsp garlic powder
- ½ tsp onion powder
- ½ tsp black pepper
- 1 tsp salt

### For Frying

- Oil, for deep or shallow frying

---

## Step 1: Marinate the Chicken

1. Add chicken to a bowl.
2. Season with salt, black pepper, chilli powder, and lemon juice.
3. Mix well, cover, and marinate for **at least 30 minutes**  
   _(overnight gives better flavour and juiciness)._

---

## Step 2: Coat the Chicken

1. In a bowl or container, mix all dry batter ingredients.
2. Add marinated chicken strips **in small batches**.
3. Toss or shake until evenly coated.
4. For extra crunch (optional):
   - Dip coated chicken briefly in cold water
   - Return to dry batter and coat again

---

## Step 3: Fry the Chicken Tenders

1. Heat oil to **170–180°C (medium-hot)**.
2. Fry chicken in batches without overcrowding.
3. Fry until **golden, crisp, and cooked through**.
4. Remove and rest on a wire rack or paper towel.

At this stage, you have **regular crispy chicken tenders**.

---

## Regular Chicken Tenders (Plain)

Serve the hot tenders as-is with:

- garlic mayo
- ketchup
- peri peri mayo
- mustard sauce

Perfect for dipping and snacking.

---

## Honey Chilli Chicken Tenders

Sticky, sweet, and spicy. minimal ingredients, big flavour.

### Honey Chilli Sauce

- 1½ tbsp **honey**
- 1 tbsp **gochujang paste**
- 1 tsp **soy sauce**
- 1 tsp oil or butter _(optional, for shine)_

### Tossing Method

1. Heat a pan on **low flame**.
2. Add oil or butter (optional).
3. Add honey, gochujang paste, and soy sauce.
4. Stir gently until combined and slightly glossy.
5. Add fried chicken tenders.
6. Toss quickly to coat evenly.
7. Turn off heat immediately to avoid over-thickening.

---

## Serving Suggestions

- Garnish with sesame seeds or spring onions (optional)
- Serve hot with fries or slaw
- Best eaten immediately while crispy

---

## Notes

- Keep sauce heat low — honey burns fast.
- Adjust gochujang for spice level.
- Sauce should coat, not drown the chicken.
