# Pan-Fried Chicken with Broccoli & Rice

A clean, simple, everyday plate — juicy pan-fried chicken, bright green broccoli, and fluffy white rice. Minimal seasoning, high comfort, and very meal-prep friendly.

---

## Ingredients (1 serving)

### Chicken

- 100g **boneless chicken breast or thigh**, cut into bite-sized pieces
- 3–4 tbsp **dahi (curd / yogurt)**
- ½ tsp **salt**
- ¼ tsp **black pepper**
- ½ tsp **red chilli powder**
- 1 tsp **oil** (for pan-frying)

> The chicken is marinated in dahi, chilli powder, black pepper, and salt for **12 hours**. You can marinate for less time, but it won't be as juicy.

### Broccoli

- 1 cup **broccoli florets**
- Salt, to taste

### Rice

- ½ cup **white rice** (basmati or short-grain)
- 1 cup water
- Salt, to taste

---

## Instructions

### 1. Cook the rice

1. Rinse the rice until the water runs clear.
2. In a pot, add rice, water(quantity depends on the type of rice actually, but 1.5x-2x volume of rice is generally suggested), and a pinch of salt.
3. Bring to a boil, then cover and cook on **low heat** for 10–12 minutes.
4. Turn off heat and let it rest, covered, for 5 minutes.
5. Fluff with a fork and set aside.

---

### 2. Cook the broccoli

1. Bring a small pot of salted water to a boil.
2. Add broccoli florets and blanch for **2–3 minutes** until bright green.
3. Drain immediately and set aside.

> Optional: Toss broccoli with some melted butter for extra flavor.

---

### 3. Pan-fry the chicken

1. Remove the marinated chicken from the fridge and let it rest at room temperature for 10 minutes.
2. Heat a pan over **medium heat** and add oil.
3. Place chicken pieces in a single layer, shaking off excess marinade.
4. Cook undisturbed for **3–4 minutes** until a golden crust forms.
5. Flip and cook another **3–4 minutes** until fully cooked and lightly charred.

---

### 4. Assemble

1. Plate a mound of rice.
2. Add pan-fried chicken on the side.
3. Finish with steamed broccoli.

---

## Notes

- Chicken thighs give more juiciness; breast keeps it lean.
- This works great for meal prep — stays good for 2–3 days refrigerated.
- Add garlic, ginger, or a light glaze if you want more punch.
