# Garlic Spaghetti with Korean Fried Chicken

A comforting East-meets-West plate — buttery garlic spaghetti with chilli flakes and herbs, paired with crispy Korean-style fried chicken coated in a sticky, spicy-sweet glaze.

---

## Ingredients (1–2 servings)

## Korean Fried Chicken

### Chicken & Batter

#### Chicken

- 200–250 g **boneless chicken** (thighs or breast), cut into chunks/strips
- Salt, to taste
- Black pepper (for marination)
- 1 lemon's juice (for marination), or 2 tbsp curd
- 1 tsp Chilli powder (for marination)

#### Batter

- quarter cup **cornflour (cornstarch)**
- one cup **all-purpose flour**
- 1 tsp coriander powder
- 1 tsp onion powder
- Black pepper, to taste
- 1 tsp salt
- Oil, for shallow or deep frying

### Korean Sauce

- 2 tbsp **gochujang**
- 1½ tbsp **soy sauce**
- 1 tbsp **ketchup**
- 1 tbsp **honey or sugar**
- 1 tsp **vinegar or rice vinegar**
- 1 tsp **garlic**, finely minced
- ½ tsp **sesame oil** (optional)

---

## Garlic Spaghetti

- 100–120 g **spaghetti**
- 2 tbsp **olive oil or butter**
- 5–6 cloves **garlic**, finely sliced
- ½ tsp **chilli flakes** (adjust to taste)
- Salt, to taste
- Black pepper, to taste
- Fresh parsley or spring onions, finely chopped

---

## Instructions

### 1. Prepare the chicken

1. Marinade chicken pieces for 12 hours with salt, black pepper and lemon juice.
2. Mix the spices, salt, cornflour, and all-purpose flour and create a dry batter.
3. Move the batter to a medium sized container, and add the chicken pieces, 3-4 at a time and close the container, then shake really well.
4. Take out the chicken pieces, and put them in another container with cold water in it.
5. Wait for half a minute, take out the chicken pieces from the water container and again put them in the batter container.
6. Shake the batter container.
7. Place the prepared chicken in a plate, after they're coated well with the batter.
8. Repeat this process for the remaining chicken pieces.

---

### 2. Fry the chicken

1. Heat oil to **medium-hot** (170–180°C).
2. Fry chicken in batches, turning occasionally, until golden and crisp.

---

### 3. Make the Korean sauce

1. In a pan over low heat, combine gochujang, soy sauce, ketchup, honey, vinegar, and garlic.
2. Cook for **2–3 minutes** until glossy and slightly thickened.
3. Stir in sesame oil if using.

---

### 4. Coat the chicken

1. Add the fried chicken to the sauce.
2. Toss quickly until evenly coated.
3. Remove from heat.

---

### 5. Cook the garlic spaghetti

1. Boil spaghetti in well-salted water until **al dente**.
2. Reserve ¼ cup pasta water, then drain.
3. Heat butter in a pan on **low heat**.
4. Add garlic slices and cook gently until fragrant (do not brown).
5. Add chilli flakes.
6. Toss in spaghetti and a splash of pasta water.
7. Season with salt and black pepper.
8. Finish with parsley or spring onions.

---

### 6. Assemble

1. Plate garlic spaghetti on one side.
2. Add Korean fried chicken alongside.
3. Serve immediately.

---

## Notes

- Chicken thighs stay juicier, but breast works well too.
- Keep garlic heat low to avoid bitterness.
- This pairing works surprisingly well — spicy, sweet, garlicky, and deeply satisfying.
