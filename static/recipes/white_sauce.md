# Creamy White Sauce Pasta with Garlic Bread

A simple, comforting white sauce pasta — creamy, mildly garlicky, and finished with chilli flakes, black pepper, and fresh basil. Served with crisp pan-toasted garlic bread on the side.

---

## Ingredients (1 serving)

### Pasta

- 100–120 g **short pasta** (fusilli / penne / rotini)
- Salt, for boiling water

### White Sauce

- 1 tbsp **butter**
- 1 tbsp **all-purpose flour (maida)**
- 1 cup **milk** (warm)
- 2–3 cloves **garlic**, finely minced
- Salt, to taste
- Black pepper, to taste
- ½ tsp **chilli flakes** (optional)
- 2 tbsp **cheese** (processed cheese or parmesan), optional but recommended

### Garnish

- Fresh **basil** or parsley
- Extra chilli flakes & black pepper

### Garlic Bread

- 2–3 slices **bread** or baguette
- Butter
- Garlic (minced or paste)

---

## Instructions

### 1. Cook the pasta

1. Bring a large pot of well-salted water to a boil.
2. Cook pasta until **al dente** according to packet instructions.
3. Reserve ¼ cup pasta water and drain the rest.

---

### 2. Prepare garlic bread

1. Mix softened butter with a little garlic and salt.
2. Spread on bread slices.
3. Toast on a pan over **medium heat** until golden and crisp on both sides.
4. Set aside.

---

### 3. Make the white sauce

1. Heat butter in a pan on **low heat**.
2. Add garlic and sauté briefly until fragrant (do not brown).
3. Add flour and cook for **1 minute**, stirring continuously.
4. Slowly add warm milk while whisking to avoid lumps.
5. Cook until the sauce thickens to a creamy consistency.
6. Season with salt, black pepper, and chilli flakes.
7. Stir in cheese if using.

---

### 4. Combine

1. Add cooked pasta to the sauce.
2. Toss gently to coat.
3. Add a splash of reserved pasta water if needed.

---

### 5. Serve

1. Plate the pasta.
2. Garnish with basil, chilli flakes, and black pepper.
3. Serve hot with garlic bread on the side.

---

## Notes

- Keep the heat low while making the sauce to avoid splitting.
- You can add mushrooms, corn, or broccoli for a veggie version.
- For extra richness, finish with a knob of butter.
