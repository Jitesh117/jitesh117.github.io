## Paneer Bhurji

A quick, comforting **paneer bhurji**, soft scrambled paneer cooked with onions, tomatoes, and simple spices. Perfect with roti, paratha, or even bread.

---

### Ingredients

- 200–250 g **paneer**, crumbled
- 2 tbsp **oil** or **butter**
- 1 tsp **cumin seeds**
- 1 medium **onion**, finely chopped
- 2 medium **tomatoes**, finely chopped
- 1 tsp **ginger-garlic paste**
- ½ tsp **turmeric powder**
- 1 tsp **red chilli powder** (adjust to taste)
- 1 tsp **coriander powder**
- Salt, to taste
- Black pepper, to taste
- Fresh **coriander leaves**, finely chopped
- Optional:
  - 1–2 tbsp **cream** or **milk** (for softer bhurji)
  - Green chilli, finely chopped

---

### Instructions

1. Heat oil or butter in a pan on **medium heat**.
2. Add cumin seeds and let them crackle.
3. Add chopped onions and sauté until soft and lightly golden.
4. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook until the raw smell disappears.
5. Add tomatoes, turmeric, chilli powder, coriander powder, and salt.
6. Cook until tomatoes soften and oil starts to separate.
7. Add crumbled paneer and mix gently.
8. Cook for **2–3 minutes**, stirring lightly.
9. Add cream or milk if using, and mix well.
10. Season with black pepper and garnish with fresh coriander.
11. Turn off the heat, do not overcook.

---

### Serving Suggestions

- Serve hot with **roti, paratha, or toast**
- Pairs well with sliced onions and lemon wedges
- Can be used as a **sandwich or roll filling**

---

### Notes

- Do not overcook paneer, it turns rubbery.
- Butter gives a richer, dhaba-style flavour.
- For extra spice, finish with a little garam masala.
